Gameplay

Full Circle MUD is a deep, text-driven multiplayer game in the tradition of CircleMUD and DikuMUD — real-time combat, player-driven economy, procedural dungeons, and AI NPCs that remember your name.

This page covers the core mechanics. Use the links below to jump to a specific section.

Getting Started

Connect via the browser client or any MUD client that supports WebSocket. Once logged in, create a character with the charcreate command. The creation wizard walks you through:

  1. Race — each race grants ability score bonuses, starting languages, and a size category
  2. Class — determines your combat role, skill access, spell schools, starting weapon, and class-specific skills
  3. Alignment — nine alignments (Lawful Good through Chaotic Evil) that gate certain classes and items
  4. Ability Scores — distribute 27 points across six abilities using a point-buy system
  5. Name — choose your character's name

You'll start with a basic weapon for your class, your class-specific skills, and a set of general skills available to all characters (such as crafting professions and battleskills).

After creation you'll enter the world. Type look to see your surroundings, exits to see where you can go, and help to see available commands.

Multiclassing

Visit a Guildmaster NPC to advance in your class or pick up a new one. Each guildmaster has a level cap — to advance further, you'll need to find a higher-tier guildmaster elsewhere in the world. Multiclassing requires meeting ability score minimums and may require a remort.

Remort

The remort system provides long-term progression. After reaching a level cap, you can remort to reset to level 1 while keeping permanent perks: extra point-buy budget, bonus HP/mana/move per level, and access to higher-tier classes and items.

Modifiers: Your ability modifier is floor((score - 10) / 2). A score of 10–11 gives +0, 14–15 gives +2, and so on. Modifiers affect combat rolls, skill checks, and resource pools.

Point buy: All abilities start at 8. You have 27 points to distribute (more after remorts). The cap before racial bonuses is 15.

{# ── Levels & Experience ── #}

Levels & Experience

The level cap is 40. XP is earned from combat (10 × mob level per kill), quests, and crafting. When you have enough XP, visit a Guildmaster and use advance to spend your pending levels.

Each level grants HP, mana, and move increases based on your class progression tables, plus skill points to spend on new abilities.

{# ══════════════════════════════════════════════════ #} {# ── Combat ── #}

Combat

Combat is real-time. Once you engage a target with attack <target>, your character automatically swings every few seconds based on weapon speed. You can queue actions between swings.

Attack Resolution

Each swing rolls d20 + hit bonus against the target's AC (armour class). On a hit, damage is rolled using your weapon's damage dice plus your ability modifier and any bonuses. A natural 20 is a critical hit, doubling the damage roll.

Advantage & Disadvantage

Some abilities grant advantage (roll 2d20, take the best) or impose disadvantage (roll 2d20, take the worst) on attack rolls. Stunned or prone targets give advantage to all their attackers.

Damage Types

Weapons and spells deal specific damage types: slashing, piercing, bludgeoning, fire, cold, lightning, acid, poison, magic, force, necrotic, radiant, and psychic. Armour and spells can grant resistance or vulnerability to specific types.

Higher mastery tiers unlock weapon-specific abilities. For example, a Grandmaster greatsword wielder gains Cleave (cascading AoE) and Executioner (free attack on kill), while a Master rapier duelist gains Riposte (counter-attack after a successful parry).

Dual-Wielding

Daggers and short swords can be dual-wielded. Equip your main weapon with wield and your off-hand with hold. Your main-hand mastery determines how many off-hand attacks you get.

Armour & Equipment

Characters have 19 wear slots for equipment:

  • Head
  • Face
  • Left/Right Ear
  • Neck
  • Cloak
  • Body
  • Left/Right Arm
  • Hands
  • Left/Right Wrist
  • Left/Right Ring Finger
  • Waist
  • Legs
  • Feet
  • Wield (main hand)
  • Hold (off-hand / shield)
Armour Class (AC)

Base AC is 10 + DEX modifier. Equipment adds or subtracts from this. Spells like Shield and Mage Armor provide temporary AC boosts. Lower AC is harder to hit.

Durability

All weapons and armour have durability that degrades through combat and over time based on material quality. Materials range from Cloth (degrades fastest) to Mithral (degrades slowest). Use the repair command at a workshop to restore durability.

Nature Magic (Druid / Ranger)

Entangle, Call Lightning, Earthquake — nature spells that interact with terrain and the environment.

How Magic Works
  1. Learn a spell by consuming a Spell Scroll item: transcribe <scroll>
  2. Memorise the spell to prepare it for casting: memorise <spell>
  3. Cast the spell at a target: cast <spell> [target]

Each casting costs mana. Your mana cap is based on level, prime ability modifier, and equipment bonuses. Mana regenerates over time — faster when resting or sleeping.

Spells scale with your mastery tier in that school. Higher mastery means more damage, more targets, longer durations, and new effects. For example, Magic Missile fires 1 missile at Basic but up to 5 at Grandmaster.

Skills & Training

Skills use the same five-tier mastery system as weapons (Basic through Grandmaster). You spend skill points earned at each level to advance. Visit a Trainer NPC and use train <skill> to advance.

Crafting Skills (all classes)
  • Blacksmith — forge weapons and armour from metal ingots
  • Carpenter — craft weapons, shields, and furniture from timber
  • Tailor — sew clothing and cloth armour from cloth
  • Leatherworker — craft leather armour and equipment from leather
  • Jeweller — craft jewellery from silver ingots and gems
  • Alchemist — brew potions from herbs and reagents
  • Enchanting — imbue items with magical properties (mage only)
General Skills (all classes)
  • Battleskills — dodge, assist
  • Alertness — perception bonus, initiative
  • Cartography — create maps of explored areas
  • Animal Handling — tame and command animals

Crafting

Crafting produces NFT items — weapons, armour, potions, and jewellery that you truly own. Each crafted item exists as a unique on-chain asset.

How to Craft
  1. Learn a recipe — buy from a Trainer NPC or find recipe scrolls
  2. Gather materials — harvest and process the required resources
  3. Visit a workshop — each craft type has a specific room
  4. Craft the item — use the room's crafting command

Resource Gathering & Processing

Gathering

Raw resources are gathered at harvesting nodes throughout the world using commands like mine, chop, harvest, hunt, fish, and forage.

Processing

Raw materials must be processed at specialised stations before they can be used in crafting. Processing is available to all players regardless of skill level — just visit the right room and use the command.

Example Supply Chains
  • Food: Harvest Wheat → Mill into Flour → Bake into Bread → Eat to restore hunger
  • Metal weapons: Mine Iron Ore → Smelt into Iron Ingot → Forge into sword/armour (NFT)
  • Leather armour: Hunt for Hide → Tan into Leather → Craft into leather gear (NFT)
  • Potions: Forage Moonpetal → Distill into Essence → Brew into potion (NFT)
  • Alloys: Mine Copper + Tin Ore → Smelt into Bronze Ingot → Forge into bronze gear

Processing costs a small gold fee per batch. Use rates in any processing room to see conversion details.

{# ── The Economy ── #}

The Economy

Full Circle MUD has a fully player-driven economy. If a shopkeeper sells it, a player harvested or crafted it. Gold flows in a circle: subscriptions create gold, players earn and spend it, sinks recirculate it, and the cycle continues.

Earning Gold
  • Kill mobs and loot their corpses
  • Complete quests
  • Sell resources to NPC shopkeepers
Spending Gold
  • Buy resources from shopkeepers
  • Crafting workshop fees
  • Resource processing fees
  • Skill training at Trainers
  • Equipment repair
  • Early release from Purgatory on death
NPC Shops & AMM Pricing

Shopkeeper prices are not scripted. They're driven by real on-ledger AMM pools (Automated Market Makers) on the XRP Ledger. Buy more of a resource and the price goes up. Sell it back and the price drops. Supply and demand in action.

CommandEffect
listSee tradeable resources with live prices
buy <amount> <resource>Buy at current AMM price
sell <amount> <resource>Sell at current AMM price
quote buy <amount> <resource>Get a price quote (2-minute expiry)
acceptExecute a quoted trade

Market prices (refreshed hourly) are also visible on the Markets page.

Banking

Your account bank is a persistent, account-level store that survives character death and is shared across all your characters. Use deposit and withdraw at any bank to move gold, resources, and items between your character's inventory and your account bank.

{# ── Death & Respawn ── #}

Death & Respawn

When your HP reaches 0, your character dies. Here's what happens:

  1. All your items, gold, and resources are placed in a corpse at the location of death
  2. You're sent to Purgatory for 60 seconds (or pay gold for early release)
  3. You lose 5% of your XP
  4. After the timer expires, you respawn with 1 HP at a cemetery room you have bound yourself to with the bind command — or if unbound, at the zone transition room where you entered the zone

Your corpse is owner-locked for 5 minutes — only you can loot it. After 5 minutes anyone can loot it. After 10 minutes it despawns entirely. Get back to your corpse quickly.

Arena & PvP

The bulk of the game is PvE (player vs environment). PvP combat is available in the Arena, and possibly other areas in the future. All PvP zones are no-death rooms — if you're defeated, you keep all your items, gold, and XP and are simply teleported out with 1 HP.

{# ══════════════════════════════════════════════════ #} {# ── Dungeons ── #}

Dungeons

While some dungeons are static, many are procedurally generated every time you enter. No two runs are the same — no walkthroughs, no memorised paths. Explore, fight, and find the boss waiting at the deepest level.

Dungeon Types
  • Instance dungeons — a private or group dungeon with a boss encounter at the end. Defeat the boss to complete the run.
  • Passage dungeons — procedurally generated corridors that connect two locations in the world. Navigate through to reach the other side.
Instance Modes
  • Solo — a private instance just for you
  • Group — shared with your party (leader + followers)
  • Shared — one instance per entrance, anyone can enter

Instances have a lifetime (typically 2 hours). After that, the dungeon collapses.

{# ── Groups & Communication ── #}

Groups & Communication

Forming a Group

Groups form through a follow chain. Use follow <character> to join someone's group. The person at the top of the chain is the leader. When the leader moves, all followers in the same room move with them automatically.

CommandEffect
follow <character>Follow a character (join their group)

Communication

Languages

Characters know Common plus any racial languages. You can speak in a specific language with say/<language> <message> (e.g. say/dwarven Hello friend). Listeners who don't know the language hear garbled text with language-specific syllables.

Available languages: Common, Dwarven, Elfish, Halfling, Celestial, Kobold, Goblin, Dragon.

Socials

50 social commands for roleplay expression: bow, wave, laugh, hug, shrug, nod, and many more. Type socials for the full list.
