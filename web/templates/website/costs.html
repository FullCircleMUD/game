Why a Subscription?

Most games monetise through microtransactions, loot boxes, or selling power. We don't. Full Circle MUD uses a simple monthly subscription because it's the only model that lets us build the game we want to build — sustainably, without selling power or player data.

The Circular Economy

Gold doesn't just flow in — it circulates. When you process resources at a mill, craft items at a forge, or pay fees at an NPC shop, you're feeding the game's gold sinks. These sinks exist to combat inflation and keep the economy healthy. Here's where that gold goes:

  • 90% is recirculated back into the game — spawned on mobs, placed in treasure chests, and seeded into dungeons for players to earn again
  • 10% goes toward running costs — hosting, LLM API services, and further development

The result is a living economy where value flows in a circle: subscriptions create gold, players earn and spend it, sinks recirculate it, and the cycle continues. The gold supply stays stable because every coin is backed by reserves and actively circulating through gameplay.

{# ── Reserve Management ── #}

Reserve Management

Every gold coin is redeemable for RLUSD, fully backed 1:1 by RLUSD and non-price-volatile yield-producing reserves like tokenised T-Bills and money market funds. We manage those reserves conservatively to ensure players can always redeem their gold:

  • 25% in liquid RLUSD — available immediately for player redemptions, no waiting period
  • 75% in liquid, price-stable, yield-producing assets — e.g. T-Bills, RLUSD lending pools, and similar instruments with no price volatility risk

Yield generated by reserve assets is general operating revenue of the FullCircleMUD entity. It is not distributed to gold holders and does not accrue based on gold holdings. Our aim is to reinvest the majority of profits back into improving the game — which may include increasing reserve collateral to support a larger earnable gold supply, funding development, or expanding infrastructure. Reinvestment decisions are made at our discretion and are documented in our monthly transparency reports.

Our Running Costs

We believe in full transparency. Here's what it costs to keep Full Circle MUD running:

Hosting & Infrastructure

Server hosting, database management, XRPL node access, and the infrastructure that keeps the game world running 24/7.

LLM Services

Our AI-driven NPCs aren't scripted chatbots — every conversation with an NPC is a real API call to a large language model. When a shopkeeper remembers your name, when a quest giver reacts to your reputation, when a boss taunts you mid-fight — that's real compute. These API costs scale with the number of active players and the depth of NPC interactions.

Development

New features, content updates, bug fixes, and the ongoing work of building a world worth living in. Full Circle MUD is an independent project built with care, not a venture-funded sprint to an exit.

Monthly Financial Reports

Once the game economy goes live at beta, we'll publish monthly financial reports covering reserve balances, gold issuance and redemptions, game cashflow, and operating expenses. Full transparency — you'll always know exactly where the money is and how it's being used.
